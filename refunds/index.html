Refund Policy

Last updated: April 23, 2026

PromptKey is sold as a direct-download macOS utility with a 7-day trial before unlock. This policy explains when refunds may be requested for the direct product.

Trial First

PromptKey includes a 7-day trial so you can test the product before unlocking the direct build. The intended path is:

  • download the app
  • test the trial in your real workflow
  • unlock only if it earns a place in that workflow

When To Contact Support First

Before requesting a refund, please contact support if you run into a real installation, activation, or compatibility problem.

Support can often help with:

  • download or install issues
  • license activation problems
  • questions about supported workflows or system behavior

Refund Requests

If PromptKey still fails for you because of a real installation, activation, or compatibility issue that support cannot resolve, you can request a refund by email.

  • Include your purchase email.
  • Describe the issue briefly.
  • If the problem is activation-related, include the last 4 characters of the license key.

What This Policy Does Not Promise

This policy does not promise support for every possible macOS setup, third-party app environment, or future system change. It also does not promise that PromptKey is the right fit for every workflow. That is why the product includes a trial before unlock.
