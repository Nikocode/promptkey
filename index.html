Questions before you buy.

Here are the practical details about compatibility, local transcription, licensing, privacy, and support.

Is PromptKey another AI writing app?

No. It is a local dictation utility for Apple silicon Macs running macOS. You speak, it transcribes on your Mac, and it sends the text back to the app you were already using.

Which Macs are supported?

PromptKey is built for Apple silicon Macs: M1, M2, M3, M4, and newer M-series MacBook Air, MacBook Pro, iMac, Mac mini, Mac Studio, and Mac Pro systems running macOS.

Does PromptKey send my audio to a hosted transcription service?

No. PromptKey is designed around local speech recognition on your Mac using downloaded model assets.

What kind of work is it good for?

Notes, writing, chat, browsers, coding, and terminal work where you want speech-to-text without changing your whole workflow around a separate assistant window.

Can I try it before paying?

Yes. Download the trial DMG, use the built-in 7-day trial, and unlock the current direct build once for $10 if it earns a place in your workflow. There is no subscription and no recurring charge.

Does PromptKey need internet to dictate?

Once the required assets are installed, the dictation loop itself runs locally on your Mac. PromptKey can still reach out occasionally for license validation after unlock.

How does unlocking work after purchase?

You buy once, activate the app, and PromptKey stores a signed local entitlement on your Mac so the direct-build unlock does not feel like an online-only rental.

What happens if the target app changes or direct paste is unsafe?

PromptKey can fall back to copying the text instead of forcing it into the wrong place, so you still keep the result without a messy insertion.

Where do privacy and support live?

The public policy pages live at /privacy/, /terms/, and /refunds/. For support, email johnoblong@proton.me with your macOS version, PromptKey version, and a short description of the issue.
